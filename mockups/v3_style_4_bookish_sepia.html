On the Stream of Consciousness

Inquiry / Discuss how Virginia Woolf utilizes the stream of consciousness technique to stretch actual time versus psychological time in 'Mrs. Dalloway'.

he ticking of Big Ben anchors 'Mrs. Dalloway' in objective, chronological time—a relentless march forward. However, Woolf employs the stream of consciousness to subvert this forward momentum, creating expansive pools of psychological time within mere seconds of clock time.

When Clarissa observes a car backfiring or a skywriting airplane, the narrative doesn't move forward linearly; it plunges deeply into the subjective consciousness of multiple observers. A single moment in June stretches to encompass decades of memories at Bourton, regrets, and existential anxieties.
